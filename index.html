What is Arbitrage?

+1.52%
Guaranteed return
Arbitrage means buying the same thing on two markets at different prices — so you profit regardless of the outcome. No prediction needed. No directional risk. One side always pays.
Two platforms
Kalshi and Polymarket both sell the same NBA Finals contract — but at different prices.
️
Cover both sides
Buy YES on the cheaper platform, NO on the other. Together they cost less than $1.
Collect $1 either way
One side always resolves to $1. You spent less — so you always profit.

Simple example

Platform A: Heads = 45¢ (implies 45% chance) Platform B: Tails = 52¢ (implies 52% chance) Buy Heads on A: 45¢ Buy Tails on B: 52¢ Total cost: 97¢ Heads wins → collect $1 from A → +3¢ Tails wins → collect $1 from B → +3¢ Guaranteed profit: +3.1% with zero outcome risk

A genuine arb has zero directional risk — you profit whether the team wins or loses. This is fundamentally different from a value bet, which requires your prediction to be correct.

The Same-Event Opportunity

Both Kalshi and Polymarket offer an identical market: "Will [team] win the 2026 NBA Finals?"

Because they are separate platforms with different users and different amounts of money, they sometimes disagree on price. When the gap is large enough to cover both sides for less than $1.00, you have a risk-free trade.

Why do prices differ?

  • Different liquidity — Kalshi's bid/ask spread creates gaps Polymarket's tighter prices don't have
  • Different user bases — different traders with different beliefs
  • Slow price discovery — news hits one platform before the other
  • Thin markets — low-probability teams can be wildly mispriced

How the Arb Works

Each contract is binary: YES pays $1 if the team wins the Finals, NO pays $1 if they don't.

YES price + NO price = $1.00 (always — one side pays $1) Buy YES on Platform A + Buy NO on Platform B: Team wins → collect $1 from A, lose NO cost Team loses → collect $1 from B, lose YES cost Arb condition: YES_cost + NO_cost < $1.00 Your profit: $1.00 − (YES_cost + NO_cost)

Worked example — Denver Nuggets

Kalshi Denver YES ask: 8.0¢ (Kalshi prices Denver at ~7.5%) Polymarket Denver YES: 9.5¢ (Polymarket prices them higher) Polymarket Denver NO: 90.5¢ (= 1 − 9.5¢) Trade: Buy YES on Kalshi @ $0.080 Buy NO on Polymarket @ $0.905 Total cost: $0.985 Denver wins → $1.00 from Kalshi → profit $0.015 Denver loses → $1.00 from Poly → profit $0.015 Return: +$0.015 / $0.985 = +1.52% guaranteed

The two positions are completely independent. Kalshi and Polymarket don't communicate. You hold contracts on both platforms that together pay $1 no matter what happens.

How to Execute an Arb Trade

1

Spot the opportunity in the Arb Scanner

Note the team, which platform gets the YES and which gets the NO, and the guaranteed return %.

2

Fund both accounts in advance

You need money ready on both platforms before you spot an arb. Funding takes days — you can't react in time otherwise.

3

Execute both legs simultaneously

Open both platforms side by side. Place the YES first, then the NO immediately. Speed is critical — the gap can close in minutes.

4

Verify total cost after execution

Confirm YES paid + NO paid < $1.00. If slippage pushed your total over $1.00, the arb has been eliminated.

5

Hold until the Finals resolve

NBA Finals end in June. One side collects $1. Do not sell early unless you're actively managing the position.

Risks & Limitations

Slippage

Kalshi's ask price is what you actually pay. If the market moves while you execute, your cost increases. Always use the live ask, not the midpoint.

Liquidity

Low-probability teams have thin Kalshi markets. Large orders fill at worse prices. Keep positions small on these.

Capital lock-up

Your money is locked until the Finals resolve in June, then Kalshi takes 1–3 more business days to withdraw. Plan accordingly.

Platform risk

Either platform could fail or resolve incorrectly. Spread across small positions rather than concentrating everything in one arb.

Rule of thumb: Only use money you could afford to lose entirely. "Risk-free" means the game outcome won't hurt you — platform risk is real.

Responsible Gambling

Prediction markets are a form of gambling. Even genuine arbitrage involves platform risk and capital lock-up. Never bet money you cannot afford to lose.
